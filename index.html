    A1C Predictive Trend Model

    A1C Predictive Trend Model

    Behavior-Driven Health Analytics ‚Ä¢ Living Dashboard ‚Ä¢ Interpretable Model

    Product Analytics Feature Engineering Forecasting Behavior Design

    Problem

    HbA1C is a lagging indicator (updates every 2‚Äì3 months). Users trying to lower A1C via lifestyle changes lack real-time feedback, receive conflicting ‚Äúhealthy eating‚Äù advice, and often lose confidence or over-restrict.

    • No immediate signal linking daily meals to A1C outcomes
    • Generic guidance fails to explain derailment patterns
    • Low adherence due to uncertainty and delayed results

    Hypothesis

    If we model A1C as a function of behavioral patterns (not individual foods), we can forecast trends before labs, identify high-leverage actions, and improve adherence via interpretability.

    • Weekly averages matter more than perfect days
    • Dinner behavior disproportionately impacts fasting glucose and A1C
    • Fiber quality, timing, and pairing modulate insulin response

    Model

    1) Inputs
    Daily food + portions + exercise (plain text, Photo, Audio)
    2) Feature Extraction
    Carbs, timing, stacking, exercise, pairing, fiber
    3) A1C Proxy
    Interpretable weighted linear model
    4) Weekly Avg
    7-day smoothing (biological realism)
    5) Forecast
    Projected curve + confidence band
    Projected A1C: A1C_projected(t) = 6.40 ‚àí 0.065t (t = weeks since start)
    Actual proxy: A1C_proxy = Œ± + Œ£(w·µ¢X·µ¢) (interpretable behavioral features)
    Weekly avg: A1C_weekly = mean(last 7 days)
    Confidence band: Best = Projected ‚àí 0.08 ‚Ä¢ Worst = Projected + 0.12

    Feature Weighting (100%)

    Feature Weight Why it matters
    Dinner carbohydrate load 25% Strongest lever via overnight glucose ‚Üí fasting glucose ‚Üí A1C
    Weekly average daily carbs (7-day) 20% A1C responds to averages, not single days
    Carb stacking events 15% Prolonged glucose exposure (e.g., rice+beans, dosa+channa)
    Meal spikes > 45g 12% Large excursions drive disproportionate glycation
    Exercise (timing & frequency) 8% Improves insulin sensitivity; blunts spikes
    Protein + fat pairing 6% Flattens insulin curve; reduces peak + duration
    Fiber Score (quality) 6% Non-starchy veg/avocado/seeds reduce glucose velocity
    Carb timing (day vs night) 4% Late carbs elevate overnight glucose risk
    Liquid carbs (milk/tea/shakes) 3% Acts as a carb multiplier on borderline days
    Recovery behavior 1% Low-carb follow-ups prevent weekly derailment
    Key product insight: Dinner control + weekly average account for 45% of model impact, enabling minimal, high-leverage behavior changes.

    Outcome

    • Stable, downward A1C trend with weekly averages remaining inside the confidence band
    • Early forecasting of crossing A1C 6.0 by mid-January (trend-based)
    • Reduced anxiety and improved adherence through interpretable ‚Äúdanger zone‚Äù alerts

    Learnings

    • Interpretability beats complexity for sustained behavior change
    • Weekly averages are the right KPI; daily noise is not actionable
    • Fiber quality (veg/avocado/seeds) outperforms ‚Äúwhole grains‚Äù for glucose stability
    • Protein must be paired with fat to reduce insulin spike duration
    • Small, repeatable rules prevent derailment better than strict restriction
